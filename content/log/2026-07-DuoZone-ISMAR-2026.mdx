The paper "DuoZone: A User-Centric, LLM-Guided Mixed-Initiative XR Window Management System," authored by Jing Qian (Tongji University / VIDA), George Xi Wang, Yunge Wen, Guande Wu, Sonia Castelo Quispe, Xiangyu Li (Brown) and Fumeng Yang (University of Maryland), was accepted to the ISMAR 2026 TVCG track, and will appear in IEEE TVCG.
 
The paper looks at a growing pain point in XR productivity: virtual displays give you a huge workspace, but manually placing, arranging, and resizing all those windows gets tedious and fatiguing fast. DuoZone takes a mixed-initiative approach that keeps users in control: people design workspace layouts through spatial zones, and a cost-model-driven LLM handles placing, ordering, and adjusting applications across scenarios. In a user study (N=16) on Apple Vision Pro, DuoZone delivered significantly faster adjustments with lower effort and cognitive load than the baseline, while scaling better as workspaces grow.
